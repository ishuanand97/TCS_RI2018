INSIGHTS:

3 / 4

INSIGHTS:

1. Delhi has the highest Respirable Suspended Particulate matter. Due to urbanization, there is air pollution from vehicle emissions, industry, construction and residential fuel burning, but Delhi has an additional disadvantage of location. The heavy concentration of particulate matter is greatly affected by meteorological conditions –in the winter; cool air causes “inversions” that stagnant the air and trap pollution close to the ground. Airflow patterns from Afghanistan and Pakistan pick up emissions as they move over the densely urbanized regions of Punjab(highest RSPM after Delhi and UP) and Haryana where farmers burn the straw in their fields and pull this pollution into Delhi. Pre-monsoon dust storms also contribute to air pollution in the region.

2. Respirable suspended particulate matter, or RSPM1 is a causative agent of mortality and morbidity. Small particles aggravate respiratory and cardiac symptoms in the short term and trigger lung cancer in the long term.

INSIGHTS:

1. Delhi again has the highest content of SPM. Particulate matter is the sum of all solid and liquid particles suspended in air, many of which are hazardous. This complex mixture contains for instance dust, pollen, soot, smoke, and liquid droplets. Though many urbanized areas have high vehicle count and increasing roadside eateries burning fuels, Delhi is land-locked and hardly has any air movement. This is the reason the level is quite often thrice or more time the level of national standards.

2. Since they are so small and light, fine particles tend to stay longer in the air than heavier particles. Fine particles are also known to trigger or worsen chronic diseases such as asthma, heart attack, bronchitis and other respiratory problems.
